There are an abundance of seo tools for small businesses. But, with limited resources and budget, small businesses can face an added challenge: finding the right tech stack that does everything they need to maintain a competitive edge in search, without breaking the bank. Solving this challenge is ‌non-negotiable. According to HubSpot’s State of Marketing report, 50% of small businesses consider their website, blog, and seo their most leveraged marketing channel. When organic search is the single biggest driver of growth, finding the right tools to do it well is essential. This guide helps business owners and seo teams decide which tools fit their goals and build a tech stack that drives measurable results. It includes recommendations for free tools to get started with today, and for when you’re ready to invest, a categorized breakdown of must-have SEO software for small business. Strong keyword research and great content will only take a business so far if the underlying structure of your website isn’t working. Technical seo tools detect crawl, speed, indexing, and metadata issues that can quietly suppress your rankings, often with no obvious “symptoms” until traffic drops. Catching technical seo issues early is far less costly than trying to recover from them later. The following tools help SEO teams capture these issues before they cause significant harm. HubSpot’s Website Grader provides a free technical audit starting point for small businesses who want a clear, jargon-free snapshot of how their website is performing. To use Website Grader, simply enter your URL and email address, and within seconds the tool provides a scored report covering performance, seo, mobile readiness, and security.

Helpfully, there’s a plain-English explanation of what each finding means and why it matters.

Unlike enterprise audit tools that surface hundreds of issues with little guidance on where to begin, Website Grader is actionable. The report includes recommendations that any seo team can act on. For small businesses doing a first audit or trying to make the case internally for seo investment, it’s one of the most accessible entry points available. It won’t replace a full technical crawl as your site grows in complexity, but it provides a fast, reliable baseline and a clear list of priorities to work from. Best for: small businesses running their first technical audit, or teams that need a quick, shareable snapshot of site health without the complexity of an enterprise crawl tool. What I like: Website Grader is free and easy to use. If budgets are tight, then seo teams can use this tool to get a snapshot of a website’s performance. Rank tracking tools measure position changes for target keywords over time, giving teams the visibility to know what’s working, what’s slipping, and where to focus optimization efforts next.

Google Search Console shows search queries, indexing status, and site performance data, all sourced directly from Google, and all completely free.

For small businesses starting out with rank tracking, Google Search Console is the most logical first tool to set up, not only because it’s free but because the data comes straight from the source.

The Performance report is the core of Search Console’s rank-tracking value. Filter by query, page, country, device, and date range, and compare periods to see whether positions are improving or declining.

The tool’s limitation, compared to paid rank tracker tools, is that it won’t provide daily position updates or the granular keyword tracking of a dedicated tool, but for a small business building its seo foundation, it provides more than enough signal to prioritize the right work. Best for: small businesses at any stage that want reliable, first-party ranking and performance data directly from Google at no cost. What I like: Google Search Console is a must-have for all businesses, small and enterprise. It’s incredibly useful, and as an seo consultant, I log into it every single day. Use “Average Position” to see how your keywords or pages are ranking. Remember: it’s average position so rankings will have fluctuated. Use filters to get the most accurate results about how specific keywords are performing and filter by the countries the website is supposed to be ranking. HubSpot AEO evaluates brand visibility in AI search results. It helps the search marketing team go beyond traditional search by giving them a clear picture of whether and how a brand is showing up when AI tools answer questions relevant to your business, products, or industry.

Think of it as a rank tracker for the AI era. Rather than monitoring a website’s position in SERPs, search teams are monitoring a website’s presence in the answers that AI engines are serving directly to potential customers. HubSpot AEO provides four metrics:

Brand Visibility Score is the headline metric and an indicator of how often a brand appears in AI answers across tracked prompts, expressed as a percentage. It provides a single, trackable number that communicates AI search presence to leadership without requiring them to understand the mechanics of how answer engines work.

Prompt Tracking solves one of the most common frustrations with AEO as a discipline: not knowing which questions to monitor. HubSpot AEO suggests prompts based on what it already knows about a business and buyers, drawing on CRM data to surface the questions that actual customers will likely ask AI engines, rather than requiring a team to guess.

Citation Analysis shows which domains and pages are being referenced in AI answers.

Sentiment Analysis adds a qualitative layer that pure visibility tracking misses. Sentiment analysis measures whether AI is describing your brand positively, negatively, or neutrally when it mentions you, on a scale from -100 to +100. This matters because if an answer engine is citing your brand in a negative context, that’s a reputation risk that traditional seo tools would never surface. What distinguishes HubSpot AEO from standalone AEO tools is the integration with the broader HubSpot ecosystem. Marketers can act on gap recommendations inside HubSpot, where they can create content or update existing pages without leaving HubSpot. For lean small business teams, the closed loop between insight and action is significant. Best for: Small businesses and marketing teams that are already managing traditional seo and want to extend that visibility tracking into AI search. HubSpot AEOl is particularly useful for those in competitive categories where AI-generated answers are influencing how customers discover and evaluate options before ever visiting a website What I like: The CRM-powered prompt suggestions genuinely set HubSpot AEO apart from the growing field of standalone AEO tools. Rather than handing a blank prompt builder, it takes the guesswork out of what buyers are searching for in ChatGPT. HubSpot uses what it already knows about your business and contacts to surface the prompts that actually matter, which means small teams spend less time on setup and more time acting on insights that apply to their specific buyers.

Pricing: HubSpot AEO is included with Marketing Hub Pro and Enterprise editions and is also available as a standalone subscription for $50/month.

When to upgrade: If your team has noticed organic traffic plateauing or declining despite solid traditional rankings, or if you’re in a category where AI Overviews and chatbot answers are visibly influencing how customers discover and evaluate solutions, HubSpot AEO makes that shift measurable and provides a clear action plan to respond to it.
